Besides the name and bust of the emperor, the face has what seems to be a random sequence of letters. 
This legend is a list of abbreviations, each standing for one title of the emperor. 
Some examples are “CEAS” for Caesar or “AVG” for Augustus – both meaning emperor.

The allegories and divinities on the reverse are used to indicate the state of the Roman Empire or a trait of the emperor. 
Some examples are peace, wealth, luck or intelligence.
